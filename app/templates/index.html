Basketball Hall of Fame Probability Predictor

Predicting the probabilty that a current or recently retired NBA player will make the Hall of Fame (HOF)

A Gradient Boosted Tree was trained on NBA player data between 1950 - 2011 to predict the probability that a current or recently retired NBA player will someday make the HOF.

Therefore, all players who were active after the 2011 season are in the prediction set, as the data goes through the 2016-2017 season and anyone who was stil active after 2011 would still be ineligible for HOF induction by the most recent year of data.

The following variables were used as predictors in the model:

Variable Description
YEARS Number of seasons played
WS Maximum win shares in a single season
THREE 3-point fields goals made per game over career
REB Rebounds per game over career
AST Assists per game over career
STL Steals per game over career
BLK Blocks per game over career
PTS Points per game over career

Additionally, the top 10 most similar players were calculated for each player in the prediction set based on the euclidean distance between his career statistics and those of historical players. This allows for another way to gauge a player's chances at making the HOF.
